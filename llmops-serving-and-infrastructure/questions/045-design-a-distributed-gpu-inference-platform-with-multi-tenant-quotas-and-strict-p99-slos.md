### Q: Design a distributed GPU inference platform with multi-tenant quotas and strict p99 SLOs.
* **Difficulty:** Principal
* **Category:** System Design
* **The 10-Second Pitch:** Use a global gateway, model-aware router, token/KV admission, isolated priority queues, warm GPU pools with continuous batching/paged cache, tenant-scoped caches/credentials, and SLO-driven autoscaling/fallback.
* **The Deep Dive:** Define SLOs by workload class—TTFT, inter-token latency, deadline-goodput, availability—and cap prompt/output/tool budgets so they are enforceable. The gateway authenticates principal/tenant, applies request and token rate limits, normalizes model aliases, and assigns class/deadline. A model-aware router considers warm replicas, adapter/prefix locality, topology, queue delay, KV headroom, and residency. Admission predicts prompt prefill cost plus maximum incremental KV and rejects, queues, or degrades before allocation. Per-class queues use weighted fair/deadline scheduling; iteration-level workers continuously batch chunked prefills and decodes without starving either.

  Quotas cover requests, input/output tokens, concurrent sequences, KV blocks, adapter residency, tool spend, and daily cost—not just QPS. Tenant identity scopes retrieval, adapters, prefix/semantic/KV caches, credentials, and telemetry. The control plane distributes signed immutable bundles `{weights, tokenizer, runtime, quantization, prompt, policy}`, warms and health-checks replicas, then canaries traffic. The data plane supports streaming cancellation, idempotent retries before commitment, and bounded queues. Overload policy is explicit: shed low priority, truncate only by contract, route to a smaller model, disable expensive tools, or return retry-after.
* **Production Reality & Tradeoffs:** Strict p99 requires headroom; maximum utilization and tail isolation are conflicting goals. Autoscale on predicted token work, queue delay, KV pressure, and SLO error budget, while accounting for multi-minute cold starts. Isolate noisy long-context and batch workloads into separate pools when fairness cannot contain them. Multi-region failover must preserve residency and capacity. Chaos-test worker loss, partial streams, allocator exhaustion, hot tenants, bad adapters, control-plane partitions, and region loss; a healthy average GPU utilization does not demonstrate an SLO-safe platform.
* **Red Flag:** Promising strict p99 while allowing unbounded context/output and FIFO scheduling.
