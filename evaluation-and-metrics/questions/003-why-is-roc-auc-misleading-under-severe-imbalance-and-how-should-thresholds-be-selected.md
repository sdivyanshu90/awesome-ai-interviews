### Q: Why is ROC-AUC misleading under severe imbalance, and how should thresholds be selected?
* **Difficulty:** Senior
* **Category:** Evaluation
* **The 10-Second Pitch:** ROC-AUC weights FPR by the huge negative population and summarizes all thresholds; a small FPR can still create an unusable number of false positives. Select thresholds from absolute error costs and capacity on representative validation data.
* **The Deep Dive:** ROC-AUC is a prevalence-invariant ranking probability, which is valuable for discrimination but not deployment utility. With one million negatives, FPR $0.1%$ still yields 1,000 false alarms; ROC can visually compress this operationally important region. PR curves expose positive predictive value and have a prevalence-dependent baseline, making them more informative for rare positives. Neither AUC selects a threshold. Estimate calibrated scores on a temporally separated validation set, compute expected utility or constrained optimization at each candidate threshold, and include downstream review capacity. For costs $C_FP,C_FN$, choose the action minimizing expected conditional loss; under calibrated probabilities and simple costs, act positive when $p > C_FP/(C_FP+C_FN)$, then adjust for intervention benefits, capacity, and shifted priors. Use confidence intervals in the narrow operating region and stress prevalence changes.
* **Production Reality & Tradeoffs:** PR-AUC cannot be compared across populations with different prevalence without context. Thresholds drift as priors, costs, or capacity change; monitor confusion counts and recalibrate. High AUC can coexist with poor calibration and unusable tails.
* **Red Flag:** Choosing the threshold closest to the ROC top-left corner without production costs, prevalence, or workload constraints.
