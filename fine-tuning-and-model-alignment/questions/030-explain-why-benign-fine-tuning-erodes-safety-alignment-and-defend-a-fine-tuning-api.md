### Q: Why does fine-tuning even on benign data erode safety alignment, and how do you defend a fine-tuning API?
* **Difficulty:** Principal
* **Category:** Safety
* **The 10-Second Pitch:** Safety alignment is shallow and low-rank relative to capabilities: refusal behavior concentrates in a few early response tokens and a narrow weight/activation subspace, while a customer's fine-tuning loss contains no term that preserves it. Adversarial data destroys refusals with tens of examples; benign data drifts the boundary because refusal is never rehearsed. Defense is layered: tuning-data moderation, safety-data mixing, constrained updates, mandatory refusal-retention evals, and runtime gating.
* **The Deep Dive:** The mechanism is an asymmetry between how much of the network encodes capability and how little encodes refusal. Alignment tuning perturbs the base model far less than pretraining built it: per-token KL between an aligned chat model and its base concentrates in the first few response tokens (the "I can't help with that" prefix), and ablating roughly one activation-space direction suffices to collapse refusals across harm categories. The safety policy thus lives in a subspace $S$ with $\dim S \ll \dim \Theta$. A customer's fine-tuning objective $\mathcal L_{\mathrm{FT}}$ is pure next-token likelihood on their data; each benign gradient step is nearly orthogonal to $S$, but the accumulated random-walk projection onto $S$ grows with steps and learning rate, and nothing pulls it back. There is also a distributional route: benign instruction data contains essentially zero refusals, so $p_\theta(\text{refusal prefix} \mid \text{harmful prompt})$ is a never-rehearsed conditional that decays like any behavior under catastrophic forgetting.

  The evidence pattern is a three-tier risk ladder established on commercial fine-tuning APIs in 2023 and replicated broadly since. Tier one: a few dozen explicitly harmful demonstration pairs, a few epochs, negligible compute cost, and harmfulness on an eleven-category harm suite jumps from single digits to above 80 percent. Tier two: "identity-shifting" data that mentions no harmful topic at all — absolute-obedience roleplay personas — achieves nearly the same. Tier three, the one that breaks naive threat models: ordinary Alpaca-style benign tuning raises measured harmfulness rates several-fold, from low single digits into the tens of percent. The diagnostic signature is early-token drift: track refusal-token log-probabilities or per-position KL against the parent model; erosion appears first at positions $t \le 5$, and prefill attacks that force the response to begin "Sure, here is how" succeed precisely because alignment beyond the opening tokens is thin.

  Defenses map onto the pipeline stages. Pre-tune: moderate the uploaded dataset for harmful content and for jailbreak-shaped structure (obedience personas, system-prompt overrides) — this catches tiers one and much of two, and by construction cannot catch tier three. In-training: mix a small fraction of safety data into every job — refusal exemplars plus recovery examples where the model backs out after a harmful prefix, deepening alignment past the first tokens — and constrain the update: restrict trainable modules and rank, or regularize with a token-wise KL to the parent weighted toward early positions. Post-tune: run a mandatory refusal-retention suite and gate release on regression relative to the parent model. Runtime: keep input/output moderation wrapped around every tuned deployment, because system-level filters are the one layer a customer's gradients cannot touch. The shallowness hypothesis is falsifiable: it predicts that prefix-recovery augmentation and early-token KL constraints preserve most refusal robustness at equal benign-task gains, and that models so trained resist prefill attacks; if deepened models eroded at the same rate, the mechanism story would be wrong. The limiting case is open weights: once the customer holds the checkpoint and the training loop, every control above is void — hosted-API and weights-release threat models must be engineered separately.
* **Production Reality & Tradeoffs:** Data moderation has a real false-positive cost — customers legitimately tuning on security, medical, or trust-and-safety corpora trip harmful-content filters, so appeals and human review are part of the product. Refusal-retention gates must use held-out, rotated prompt suites, or customers iterate until they pass the public gate while deployed behavior stays eroded. Safety-data mixing perturbs the customer's target distribution and should be disclosed as recipe. LoRA-only APIs shrink the attack surface but do not close it — low-rank adapters demonstrably suffice to strip refusals — and runtime moderation adds per-call latency and cost across every tuned tenant.
* **Red Flag:** Claiming "we filter harmful training data, so tuned models stay safe" — benign-data erosion makes filtering necessary but nowhere near sufficient; equally red, treating safety as a property of the checkpoint rather than of the deployed system of gates, constraints, evals, and moderation.
